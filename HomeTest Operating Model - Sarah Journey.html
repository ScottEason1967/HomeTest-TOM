A commissioner's story through the operating model

Sarah signs once.
Not once a quarter.

Sarah commissions sexual health for her Local Authority. UKHSA data shows an HIV detection gap concentrated in the wards her service struggles hardest to reach. She has the budget. She does not have the headcount to design a bespoke procurement around home self-testing for every supplier on the market. This is the story of one commissioner, one platform, one signature, and an operating model that does the procurement work for her.

The North Star
Easy buying. So the buying gets out of the way and the population outcome stays in focus.
Commissioners do not buy home testing for the joy of running procurements. They buy it to close a detection gap, reduce late diagnoses, or get more out of a stretched service. The operating model exists so the buying stops being the work. Easy buying is the North Star, not because procurement should be lazy, but because effort spent on procurement is effort not spent on population health.
Why one platform matters to her
One framework. One signature. One platform she can come back to as her cohorts and questions change.
A bespoke HIV-only procurement would buy a single deployment. The next cohort (chlamydia, syphilis, the next configuration the platform admits) would mean another procurement, another assurance set, another contract, another integration. The framework means Sarah signs once, the same NHS Login carries her cohorts, the same MI feed answers the same questions, and the next configuration is an extension, not a new programme. The integration was done before she walked in.
01
The brief · a population, a gap, twelve months to move

Sarah has an HIV detection gap she can see on the UKHSA dashboard. The late diagnoses are concentrated in MSM under-25s and the wards her clinics struggle hardest to reach. The Health and Wellbeing Board wants outcomes. She has twelve months and the LA sexual health budget. She does not have the headcount to bespoke a procurement around every supplier on the market.

I can see the gap on the dashboard. I've got money on the table and the Board wants to see movement inside a year. What I don't have is the headcount to bespoke a procurement for every supplier.

Sarah
What the model is doing underneath
UKHSA late-diagnosis data showing the cohort concentrated in specific wards. A Health and Wellbeing Board paper asking what digital does for the gap. The LA sexual health budget. An existing sexual health service contract with the local provider. The clinical case is settled: home self-testing for HIV removes the clinic-visit barrier that keeps the late-diagnosed cohort away. What's in front of her is procurement, integration with the existing service, and reach into the wards that don't engage.
The old way
A bespoke procurement against the LA's contract regulations. Six to nine months. Clinical safety assurance per supplier. IG assessment per supplier. Service-contract amendment to carry the new pathway. Twelve to fifteen months from Board paper to first kit, on a good run. The last new pathway she stood up before the framework existed took twenty-two months. That is a year her late-diagnosed patients did not have.
👥
What the care team sees
Her sexual health service provider lead. Her LA Clinical Safety Officer. Her IG lead. The public-health intelligence team who built the cohort analysis. Procurement, not yet engaged because the route is not yet chosen.
The money behind Sarah's budget
Her sexual health envelope traces back to the DHSC Public Health Grant. LAs receive it, choose whether to run sexual health themselves or commission it out, and typically retender the whole Tier-3 service (in-person consultation plus at-home testing plus outreach) as a bundle. The bundled tender is where the money squeeze bites. Testing budgets get cut inside the bundle before the in-person consultation budget does; it's easier to trim tests than to trim consultant sessions. LA cycles run three to four years on average. National spend across the LA sexual health market runs at around £547m (2022/23). Two suppliers (Preventx and SH:24) hold roughly 80% of the LA HIV self-testing arrangements today. Sarah's Preventx contract runs until March 2029 — her framework call-off window is contract-cycle limited, not decision limited.
02
One front door · the framework opens

She opens the framework. The HomeTest platform is admitted. The HIV configuration is live. Suppliers are listed against published call-off terms. Templates ready. National pricing visible. No procurement to design from scratch.

I expected to be writing a spec. I'm looking at suppliers I can already call off. The framework did the heavy lifting before I logged in.

Sarah
What the model is doing underneath
The framework page. HIV self-test suppliers admitted to the HomeTest platform. Standard call-off terms. DSA, DPIA, privacy notice and clinical safety case starter templates pre-built. NHS Login carries the cohort. Targeting and reach capabilities exposed via UKHSA data overlays and NHSE social channels. The route is published; she just has to choose to take it.
The old way
A clean-sheet procurement compliant with the LA's contract regulations. Spec writing. Supplier engagement. Two or three rounds of clarifications. A standstill period. Bespoke clinical safety case from a blank page. Bespoke IG. Bespoke MI. Three months gone before the first contract draft.
👥
What the care team sees
The platform's commissioner-engagement contact for the configuration. Her LA procurement lead, sense-checking the call-off route. Her sexual health service provider lead, sense-checking how the pathway plugs into the existing service.
What the national arrangement actually gets her on price
The framework carries national pricing negotiated at scale, not per-LA. Kit unit cost sits in a documented £6-10 band; a self-sample home test typically lands at £15-20 to the commissioner. The comparator that anchors the case is not another self-sample supplier — it's the first outpatient sexual health appointment (£162) and the follow-up (£108) she would otherwise pay for. Sarah is buying two things at once through the framework: national unit price power that a single LA cannot achieve on its own, and channel shift out of the in-person tariff. The framework arrangement runs to 28 October 2029 under SBS10255 — same cycle length as her bundled Tier-3 tender, but on a national assurance base she doesn't have to build.
03
Fit for HER cohort · the questions standard answers don't solve

Standard answers don't help her if her late-diagnosed patients are exactly the ones standard provision misses. Unsafe-address households. Low digital literacy. Reach into the wards her clinics don't see. She runs her open questions against the framework, expecting friction.

Standard answers don't help me if my late-diagnosed patients are exactly the ones standard provision misses. I need to know how this works for the people I'm trying to reach.

Sarah
What the model is doing underneath
Her open questions list against the framework's published outputs. The unsafe-address pathway: how kit goes to a patient whose home address is not a safe delivery point. The low-digital-literacy path: an alternative entry route for patients who can't engage with an app-led journey. The ward-level outreach overlay: how UKHSA data and NHSE social channels reach the cohorts her clinic doesn't see. The framework either answers these or it doesn't. The platform either has the capability or it doesn't. She finds out before signing.
The old way
Six rounds of meetings with each supplier to discover what they actually do for her cohort. Bespoke service design negotiated supplier by supplier. Pilot phase running into year two. Or, in the worst case, skip the question entirely and assume what is on offer is fine. The pattern from her last new pathway: assumed it would work for everyone, found out at month nine that the cohort she was targeting hadn't engaged.
👥
What the care team sees
Her public-health intelligence lead on the cohort. Her sexual health service's outreach lead. The platform's engagement contact on unsafe-address policy and the alternative entry route. Her safeguarding officer.
04
One call-off · signing, not procuring

The call-off goes in. Six weeks from brief to live, not eighteen months. The LA's sexual health service contract carries the home-testing pathway as an extension. NHS Login carries the cohort. Payment via published call-off terms. The procurement scar she carried in is not the procurement she ran.

I've signed call-offs that took twenty minutes. The eighteen months I didn't spend negotiating is the eighteen months my patients didn't wait.

Sarah
What the model is doing underneath
The call-off itself. The supplier admitted to the platform under the framework. The local sexual health service contract amended to carry the new pathway as an extension. DSA executed against the framework's template. Clinical safety case approved by the LA's CSO using the framework's starter case as the spine. First batch of kits ordered. Outbound communications drafted for the wards the public-health team flagged. Every step has a published template. Nothing is being designed in the moment.
The old way
A multi-year bilateral contract with each supplier. Bespoke pricing schedule. Bespoke clinical safety case from a blank page. Bespoke IG. Bespoke MI. Each adding months to the deployment timeline. Each reducing the budget left for the actual outreach.
👥
What the care team sees
Her LA procurement lead. Her LA CSO. Her IG lead. The sexual health service provider lead. The platform's engagement contact handling the call-off process. The supplier's account manager.
The design lever · ring-fenced digital envelope
The framework doesn't solve the bundled-tender squeeze on its own. If the LA still bundles home-testing spend inside the Tier-3 in-person contract, home testing still gets cut first when the bundle tightens. The design move that lands the framework properly is a ring-fenced digital envelope inside the LA contract — home-testing spend held separately from the in-person consultation budget, so future budget pressure doesn't drift the two lines against each other. LAs have done this in practice: East Sussex has been running a ring-fenced digital line for several years, and it's what protects innovation capacity when the wider sexual health envelope tightens. Sarah's call-off is cleaner because she's carrying a ring-fenced line rather than a bundled one.
05
Live operation · kits out, results back

Quarter one live. Kits going out to the wards her public-health team targeted. Self-test results triaged through her existing sexual health service. The MI feed shows reach into the cohorts standard provision missed. The pathway just works for the patient.

Patients don't see procurement. They see a kit in the post and a follow-up from a service they trust. The pathway just works for them.

Sarah
What the model is doing underneath
The MI dashboard. Kits ordered. Kits delivered. Results returned (positive, negative, invalid). Triage decisions. Follow-up clinic bookings. Reach into the target wards versus the LA baseline. Equity reach against the LA's inequality targets. Reactive support paths for unsafe-result delivery. The numbers are visible in real time. She doesn't need to wait for the annual return.
The old way
Hand-rolled MI. Manual chasing of result returns. Manual follow-up. No view of equity reach until the annual report. The risk: finding out a year late that the cohort the LA targeted hadn't actually engaged, by which time the next year's budget had been set on the assumption it had.
👥
What the care team sees
The sexual health service provider running the clinical pathway. The supplier running the kit logistics. The platform running the data layer. The LA outreach team running ward-level engagement. Sarah herself looking at the MI feed weekly.
06
The board paper · what the members see

First-year outcomes. HIV detection rate up. Late-diagnosis rate down. Equity reach into the wards her clinics didn't see, verified. The board paper writes itself. The next year's ask is for the next configuration, not the next procurement.

The Health and Wellbeing Board wanted to know whether it worked. The numbers do most of the talking. The pathway behind them is what made the numbers possible.

Sarah
What the model is doing underneath
HIV detection rate against the LA baseline. Late-diagnosis rate, by ward. Equity reach into the cohorts the LA targeted. Cost per detection. Clinic capacity released back to the service for follow-up and treatment. Patient feedback. Reactive-pathway performance for unsafe-result delivery. The recommendation for year two: extend to the next configuration the platform admits. Same framework. Same call-off shape. Same MI feed.
The old way
A board paper hedged with ‘data still maturing’ and ‘next year we will have a fuller picture’. Three years before a programme of this size could be evaluated against equity targets. By which time the LA had moved on, the funding had been re-prioritised, or the member who sponsored it had left office.
👥
What the care team sees
The Health and Wellbeing Board. The Director of Public Health. Elected members on the relevant scrutiny committee. The LA finance team setting the next year's commissioning envelope.
The board paper economics
The unit-cost line is honest: at £15-20 per self-sample versus £162 for the first outpatient appointment she avoided, the channel-shift saving is the loud number. The subtle number is cost per detection — early detection against the LA baseline, with the wards her clinics did not reach now visible on the MI feed. The subtler number still is the per-detection saving in downstream treatment and clinic capacity. The board paper writes itself because the framework's MI feed generated the comparators in real time; she isn't hand-modelling anything against a stale baseline. The next year's ask isn't another procurement — it's the ring-fenced digital envelope written into the next Tier-3 tender.
Sarah's win

Detection earlier in the wards her clinics did not reach. Late diagnoses down. Equity verified.

The patients didn't see a procurement. They saw a kit in the post and a follow-up call from the sexual health service. The eighteen months Sarah didn't spend on the back office is the eighteen months her patients didn't spend undiagnosed. The next configuration is an extension of the same signature, not a new programme from a clean sheet.

WHEN

Two loops, four beats

The rhythm by which the model itself gets better — adapt, implement, review, learn

There are two loops in the operating model, running at the same time on different clocks. The inner loop is the rhythm of doing — the five-stage operational cycle (Shape, Assure, Contract, Fulfil, Measure). The outer loop is the slow turn by which the model itself gets better — adapt the standards and framework, implement the changes, review what they produce, learn the patterns, then adapt again. Plan, Do, Check, Act — the continuous-improvement cycle, applied to the model itself. The model is the relationship between the two loops.

The inner loop · The cadence
The rhythm of doing
The repeating beat of the work itself. Its shape is the business: for the NHS DTx platform that is supplier admission cycles, governance reviews, contract management, service delivery and operational measurement. The loop you can feel; the one with a calendar.
On this model: the inner loop is run by the platform and the suppliers. NHSE convenes it; it is the operational pentagon below.
The outer loop · The renewal
The loop of improving
The slow turn by which the model gets better: adapt → implement → review → learn. Plan, Do, Check, Act. What each cycle reveals feeds back and changes the next. The part that makes the model alive rather than fixed.
On this model: the outer loop is where NHSE earns its keep. Quiet today; gets loud as configurations multiply and patterns surface.
↻ The two loops, in picture

The outer loop is drawn around the pentagon as a slow turning halo with four beats — adapt, implement, review, learn — at the compass points. The inner loop is the pentagon itself. Their relative speeds are deliberate: the inner turns several times per outer turn.

What lives in each stage

DDTx 26/27 outcomes mapped onto the five lifecycle stages, with current programme activity called out.
Stage 1
Shape
Strategy and design. Where the operating model itself gets defined and the architecture decisions are made.
What this stage does
Designs the operating model. Defines the foundation, the overlays, the patterns. Sets the strategic direction for which conditions are prioritised and how the platform scales beyond HIV.
Active programme work
009Future-state operating model definition (the four-level model). Pattern catalogue. Procurement Options Matrix design. 010Use Case 3 design (focused on relieving GP pressures).
In flight
Procurement Options Matrix now covers four options including Digital Primary Care Framework as the fourth option. Growth strategy deck under review for alignment.
Open
Tension between two framings in play: a strategic framing favouring PSR, with central orchestration rejected and a register-then-framework sequencing; and the Commercial workstream matrix with PA23 confirmed and four mutually exclusive options. The two need reconciling before either anchors externally.
Stage 2
Assure
Clinical safety, IG, accreditation. The work that makes the service safe and lawful before it goes live.
What this stage does
Confirms that each pattern can be delivered safely and legally. Maintains the clinical safety case, the hazard log, the IG framework, the DPIA, the accreditation register. Assesses suppliers against published standards.
Active programme work
DPIA in progress. CATR for HIV private beta in progress. Clinical safety case for Use Case 1 (HIV) submitted to Clinical Safety Group (CSG). WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility plan. Hazard log maintained.
In flight
Use Case 2 (PSA acute initiated) clinical safety artefacts being mapped (CRMP, draft HL, draft CSCR). Supplier assurance framework for HomeTest at-scale. MHRA / DTAC assessment for the HomeTest platform.
Open
Whether the Digital Primary Care Framework (if adopted) carries an assurance level appropriate for HomeTest, or whether HomeTest would inherit the Remote Monitoring service category's assurance bar, which is lower than HomeTest-grade.
Stage 3
Contract
Procurement and supplier agreements. The vehicle through which the centre and local systems engage suppliers.
What this stage does
Selects the procurement vehicle. Awards to suppliers. Executes call-offs. Operates call-off agreements between commissioners and suppliers under the framework instrument. Sets commercial terms, payment mechanisms and tariff treatment.
Active programme work
003Alpha and Private Beta only, interim arrangements that retire when the formal framework lands: NHSE↔LA MOUs (Salford, East Sussex) and NHSE↔Supplier ACE Framework call-off via SBS Lot 1 SBS10255. Procurement Options Matrix covering four candidate routes (PSR/PA23 Framework, PA23 Dynamic Market, Notify-style central, Digital Primary Care Framework).
In flight
SBS being explored on two tracks: as a six-month PSR bridge interim for HIV, and as one possible host for the longer-term framework. Procurement regime not settled in law: NHS Legal confirms a case can be made for either PA23 or PSR (26 May 2026). Working dual-route recommendation in draft Recommendation paper: Plan A PA23 framework or Dynamic Market (Hill Dickinson external legal workshop w/c 1 June 2026), Plan B Digital Primary Care Framework leverage, Plan C commissioner-led direct. SLT alignment at 16 June Leeds playback. Royal Mail Market Reach engagement on logistics tariff classification.
Open
Recommendation between O1 (PSR or PA23 Framework), O2 (PA23 Dynamic Market), O3 (Notify-style central) and O4 (DPC Framework). PA23 Dynamic Market leads on procedural fit. DPC Framework is the only option with a clean tick on April 2027 deliverability. Notify-style central orchestration has been ruled out as a steady-state route.
Stage 4
Fulfil
Day-to-day operations. The actual delivery of the service to patients.
What this stage does
Operates the live service. Patient orders kits via NHS App. Suppliers fulfil orders. Lab analyses samples. Results return through NHS App or via clinician depending on the configuration. Logistics and physical supply chain run continuously.
Active programme work
001800,000 people able to order a free HIV test via NHS App. 00210,000 HIV home test kits ordered. 003Private Beta in 2 LAs (HIV via Salford with SH:24, East Sussex with Preventx). NHS Login, NHS Notify integrations. Kit logistics via SH:24 and Preventx (live for HIV).
In flight
Product app build for NHS App ordering surface (August timeline pressure flagged). Use Case 2 (PSA acute initiated) private beta in 2 trusts. Supplier onboarding model (self-service onboarding, a deliverable shared across Architecture, Supplier and Commercials workstreams). Royal Mail logistics engagement.
Open
Order of operations between the operating model definition and the product roadmap. Operating model first, product roadmap second so the foundation plus overlays drives what the app builds rather than the app build constraining the operating model.
Stage 5
Measure
MI, outcomes, feedback. The data that tells us whether the operating model is working and what to change next.
What this stage does
Captures uptake, return rates, sample quality, turnaround, equity, clinical outcomes. Feeds the data into the next round of Shape. Closes the loop.
Active programme work
KPI framework definition. Reporting Service Technical Spec. Data flows from suppliers into the platform. PMO holds cross-cutting MI and benefits log.
In flight
Working measures of success: tests live with approved Blue Book entries; suppliers assured by test type; systems live and stably contracting locally; sample quality (rejection / repeat-kit rates); turnaround (order to actioned result); equity of uptake (deprivation, ethnicity, age, disability).
Open
Whether the Programme Outcomes (001 to 010) are the unit of measurement, or whether the operating model carries its own measurement frame separate from the outcomes. The two should be reconciled before public reporting.

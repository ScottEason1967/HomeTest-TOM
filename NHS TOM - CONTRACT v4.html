About this stage

Suppliers are contracted tightly. Commissioners buy easily.

Same asymmetry as ASSURE. Suppliers are held to commercial terms: pricing constraints, service levels, exit provisions. Commissioners get a simplified buying experience: call-off from frameworks, clear pricing, minimal procurement overhead.

Framework structure: The GPIT three-layer contracting construct is the candidate model: catalogue agreement (standards and requirements suppliers must meet), framework agreement (national terms, max pricing, supplier entry and exit rules), call-off agreement (local commissioner-supplier direct contract). Well-established at government level but no recent NHS England precedent for NHSE running a dynamic framework itself.

Procurement routing: PA23 vs PSR not settled in law. NHS Legal confirms a case can be made for either (26 May 2026); the recommended approach is to start from the desired solution and work backwards. Working two-track recommendation: Plan A PA23 Option B framework or Dynamic Market (external procurement legal counsel formally instructed to run a scoping workshop); Plan B Digital Primary Care Framework leverage; Plan C commissioner-led direct purchase. Six-month NHS SBS Sexual Health Framework (SBS10255) recommended as the interim route for HIV only. Provisional; Confirmed at the 22 June 2026 SLT Leeds playback.

Two-track recommendation · current working position: The procurement vehicle decision lands across two tracks. Plan C (interim): NHS SBS Sexual Health Framework (SBS10255) as the Plan C interim vehicle for HIV; Alpha/Beta MOUs continue to cover live deployments. Plan A (long-term): PA23 is the recommended procurement regime per the Recommendation paper, supported by NHS Legal. Dual-route working position: Plan A NHSE-administered Open Framework under PA23 (long-term, earliest go-live August 2027); Plan C commissioner-led direct via existing frameworks (interim, covers April 2027). Plan B (Digital Primary Care Framework leverage) was discounted at playback. Framework administration: NHSE direct, SBS, CCS or third-party; sits inside Plan A. Confirmed at the 22 June 2026 SLT Leeds playback. The earlier four-option matrix (O1-O4 against 15 criteria) is historical context; criteria are reusable as Plan A narrows. Plan A earliest go-live August 2027; Plan C interim covers the April 2027 commitment.

Broader driver · tariff conversation at SLT level. The Options Matrix sits inside a wider conversation about how HomeTest pathways are funded at scale, currently live at SLT level alongside NHSE strategic finance. The procurement vehicle question (which PA23 instrument) and the tariff question (best-practice pathway tariff design) are separate workstreams but interlocked: a procurement route that assumes long-run test-economics funding will not survive the tariff conclusion. The lock target is the procurement question; the tariff question runs on its own SLT cadence and is not gated by this matrix.

Tariff planning window and 2027/28 availability. Planning guidance for the relevant tariff cycle lands October to November 2026, with health-economics modelling having to land in August to September to feed it. The primary-care contractual mechanism is not available within the 2027/28 window. The route is too contentious to use in the time available. The practical opportunity for the 2027/28 cycle sits with PSA Active Surveillance via a best-practice tariff. HomeTest configurations that depend on a primary-care funding route fall outside this window and require an alternative funding articulation.

Open Q for SLT · pathway-level economics
The commissioner case lands at pathway level, not test level. Cost and benefit sit on different budget lines. Pathology networks do not run phlebotomy; the saving falls with clinical services or the ICB while the new test cost falls with another holder. A commissioner already funding venepuncture cannot fund the switch out of test economics alone. The test reads as a cost pressure on top of an existing line. The unlock is best-practice tariffs at the pathway level, costed end-to-end against the condition pathway. Tariff design itself sits outside this operating model as a separate workstream; the dependency does not. Owner: SLT with NHSE strategic finance, Health Economist input required.
Open Q for SLT · pathology lab dual position
Where do pathology contracts live? Inside the HomeTest framework as suppliers, or outside it as clinical infrastructure HomeTest plugs into? Affects procurement vehicle, tariff treatment, exclusivity terms. Aisha's persona page treats pathology as both a supplier and a clinical infrastructure partner; the contract architecture has not yet picked a side. Owner: SLT.
Open Q for SLT · pharmacy-led prescribing
Where does pharmacy-led prescribing fit in the framework? Different governance regime (Community Pharmacy Contractual Framework), different tariff implications, different IG flows between GP and pharmacy systems. Holding position: sub-area of Aisha's clinical governance pending input. Owner: SLT. Real positioning to be agreed once the GP-init journeys (e.g. Layla's iron deficiency anaemia) are operationally specified.

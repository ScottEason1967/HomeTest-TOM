Why Abdul is here Abdul is the CVKM screening patient, the patient-initiated archetype. He is the proof point that the platform serves a patient with no diagnosis yet, no pathway open and no prescription waiting. CVKM screening is distinct from HIV testing in commissioning terms (CVKM sits in primary-care / cardiovascular commissioning; HIV LA sits with Sarah's sexual-health budget). The two are sometimes confused because both are patient-initiated through the NHS App. Abdul anchors the patient-initiated pattern; the HIV-specific patient archetype is not yet named in the model. The casting is deliberate: Abdul is the vision archetype and HIV is the live configuration. His journey is the national CVKM future-state, where patient-initiated testing is heading; HIV is the signed Beta that proves the same pattern commercially today. His full story is told in Abdul's journey, four acts drawn from the original CVKM patient journey.

Why this role matters

Abdul is the patient-initiated screening archetype. He used the NHS App himself to check his BP, the result flagged him as high cardiovascular risk, and that triggered a structured CVKM (cardiovascular, kidney, metabolic) Check Up pathway: BP validation with an AF check, bloods confirming high cholesterol and elevated cardiometabolic risk markers, a 24-hour ABPM smart ring confirming Stage 1 hypertension, treatment with statins / GLP-1 / ACE inhibitors plus referral to the pre-diabetes prevention programme, then long-term remote monitoring through home BP and continuous glucose monitoring. He represents the screening journey: the patient initiates, the home test surfaces the risk, the clinical pathway picks up.

What Abdul tells the model. Patient-initiated screening is the entry point most likely to scale at population level. It has to work without a clinician request, without a pre-existing care relationship, and without losing clinical safety. If the model can serve someone with no diagnosis yet, no pathway open, no prescription waiting: it can serve patient-initiated patterns at scale. CVKM is the first proof; Abdul is the first patient.

What's different about Abdul's journey

Abdul's journey starts before any clinician is involved. He's not on a pathway when he opens the NHS App; he's curious about his BP because heart trouble runs in the family. The home test (BP via the camera) is the entry point, not a step inside an existing care plan. The result triggers the pathway: and from that point on, the model follows the same architecture as everyone else's: validation, blood, ABPM, treatment, monitoring. The patient-initiated overlay is what makes Abdul's journey distinctive; the rest of the foundation is shared with Mark and Layla. This is why patient-initiated needs the lightest possible entry friction: the patient hasn't agreed to be a patient yet.

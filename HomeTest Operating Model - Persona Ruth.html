What the monitoring-gap cohort is

The established PSA Active Surveillance cohort covers men on pre-treatment active monitoring for low-grade prostate cancer, anchored on Mark's journey. A separate radiotherapy follow-up cohort covers men in post-treatment PSA surveillance, monitored for biochemical recurrence. The monitoring-gap cohort is distinct: men referred to Urology with an elevated PSA but no cancer diagnosis. Today these men sit between primary-care and secondary-care responsibility. The team scoping this cohort are working through how it gets identified, who clinically owns them, what the surveillance protocol looks like, and which budget pays for the monitoring.

Working hypothesis on what Ruth needs

  • A clear budget owner. If the cohort sits between primary and secondary care, the budget question has to be answered before procurement can run. The model can't name Ruth's institution until the monitoring-gap cohort discovery resolves the funding source.
  • A clinical pathway she can defend. The surveillance protocol for men with elevated PSA but no diagnosis is being developed by the team scoping this cohort. Ruth will want clinical sign-off on the protocol before she commits a budget to it.
  • Assurance she can lean on. Same baseline as Sarah and the wider commissioner set: pre-assured suppliers, clinical safety evidence, DCB0129 and DCB0160 covered, accredited labs.
  • A buy mechanism that fits. Once the budget owner is clear, the framework or dynamic market shape will determine whether this is a call-off or a bespoke arrangement.
  • An outcome she can show. The monitoring-gap case for funding rests on early detection of the patients in this cohort who go on to develop diagnosable prostate cancer. Outcome measurement still to be defined alongside the protocol.
What is held open until the monitoring-gap cohort design lands Five specifics are deliberately not named.
  • Commissioning institution. Acute trust, ICB, primary-care commissioning, joint commissioning arrangement, or central NHSE position. All five are live possibilities.
  • Budget source. Acute provider tariff envelope, ICB pathway budget, primary-care contract mechanism, or new funding earmarked specifically for monitoring-gap surveillance.
  • Clinical ownership. Whether the cohort sits with Urology, primary care, a shared monitoring service, or a new clinical owner.
  • Protocol cadence. Whether monitoring is annual, biannual, risk-stratified, or symptom-led.
  • Contract shape. Until budget owner and clinical ownership are clear, the commercial vehicle and contract structure can't be designed.

Why this role matters now

The PSA Active Surveillance configuration is the live clinician-initiated case on HomeTest. If the monitoring-gap cohort design lands without a clear commissioner buying for it, that cohort never reaches the platform regardless of how well the rest of the model is built. Putting Ruth into the cast as a TBC role signals where the dependency sits and surfaces the open questions early enough to flow into the configuration design work.

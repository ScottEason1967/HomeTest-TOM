The cast · meet Abdul

Abdul.

Patient · CVKM screening
“I checked my BP on the NHS App because heart trouble runs in the family. The app told me to book a CVKM Check Up.”
38, married, two young kids. Both he and his wife work full-time. Family history of heart disease and diabetes. Sat all day, knows his diet isn't great. Digitally savvy: expects to manage his health through one app. Used the NHS App BP tool to check his cardiovascular risk; the result triggered a CVKM Check Up pathway.

Why this role matters

Abdul is the patient-initiated screening archetype. He used the NHS App himself to check his BP, the result flagged him as high cardiovascular risk, and that triggered a structured CVKM (cardiovascular, kidney, metabolic) Check Up pathway: BP validation, blood for cholesterol and HbA1c, ABPM ring, treatment with statins / GLP-1 / ACE inhibitors, then long-term remote monitoring through home BP and CGM. He represents the screening journey: the patient initiates, the home test surfaces the risk, the clinical pathway picks up.

What Abdul tells the model. Patient-initiated screening is the entry point most likely to scale at population level. It has to work without a clinician request, without a pre-existing care relationship, and without losing clinical safety. If the model can serve someone with no diagnosis yet, no pathway open, no prescription waiting: it can serve patient-initiated patterns at scale. CVKM is the first proof; Abdul is the first patient.

What's different about Abdul's journey

Abdul's journey starts before any clinician is involved. He's not on a pathway when he opens the NHS App; he's curious about his BP because heart trouble runs in the family. The home test (BP via the camera) is the entry point, not a step inside an existing care plan. The result triggers the pathway: and from that point on, the model follows the same architecture as everyone else's: validation, blood, ABPM, treatment, monitoring. The patient-initiated overlay is what makes Abdul's journey distinctive; the rest of the foundation is shared with Mark and Layla. This is why patient-initiated needs the lightest possible entry friction: the patient hasn't agreed to be a patient yet.

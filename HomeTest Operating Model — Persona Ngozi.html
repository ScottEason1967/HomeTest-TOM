The cast · meet Ngozi

Ngozi.

Information Governance · DPO
“Tell me who the controller is, tell me the lawful basis, and we move. Until then, this doesn't go live.”
NHS England Data Protection Officer and IG lead for the HomeTest platform. Holds the framework that lets patient data flow safely between the patient, the platform, the supplier, and the clinical record. Has the authority to hold up a go-live if the data flow isn't right.

Why this role matters

Without IG, the data can't flow. Ngozi represents the framework that makes patient data legal, safe, and auditable as it moves between the NHS App, the supplier, the lab and the clinical record. The IG layer is the second framework, alongside clinical safety. Aisha makes sure the clinical activity is safe. Ngozi makes sure the data activity is lawful. The model needs both to hold for any configuration to deploy.

What sits in Ngozi's territory

Ngozi's territory is the data side of every configuration. It covers the IG artefacts that have to be produced before deployment, the lawful basis position that lets the platform process patient data, the controller designation that decides who carries the legal risk, and the operational IG processes that have to run continuously once a configuration is live. Six things sit explicitly under her care.

DPIA, DSA, DPA: the IG artefact stack
A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for every new configuration. Data Sharing Agreements (DSAs) between each pair of parties (patient consent, commissioner-to-supplier, supplier-to-platform, platform-to-record). Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) where a processor is appointed. These are the artefacts that have to land before any deployment goes live. Assure once, reuse nationally applies here too: standard DSA templates published, supplier completes once, deploys across commissioners without restarting the IG paperwork each time.
Controller designation and lawful basis
For every data flow on the platform, somebody is the data controller. The choice is consequential: the controller carries the legal risk, signs the lawful basis, and is accountable for the DPIA. The federated multi-controller model has NHSE as controller for the foundation (NHS App, NHS Notify identity), the commissioner as controller for the testing purpose, and the supplier as processor. The exact controller split per configuration is still being scoped, and joint controller arrangements have been ruled out.
IAO appointment and the Information Asset Register
Every information asset on the platform needs an Information Asset Owner (IAO) appointed under NHSE governance. The IAO is responsible for the data held in their asset, the access controls applied, and the audit trail. NHSE policy requires the IAO to be a permanent NHSE employee. The Information Asset Register (IAR) lists every asset, its IAO, its retention period, its access rules, and its lawful basis.
Open Q for SLT: IAO appointment as a critical blocker
The programme is heavily contractor-led. The IAO has to be a permanent NHSE employee. No eligible IAO has yet been identified. Without one, the platform cannot be formally signed off as ready to process live patient data. This is the single biggest IG blocker on the timeline.
Subject access requests, breach notification, Caldicott principles
The operational IG processes that have to run continuously once a configuration is live. Subject access requests routed through the right controller. Breach notification within statutory timescales. Caldicott principles applied to every use of patient-identifiable information. The seven Caldicott principles (justify, use only when necessary, minimum required, need-to-know, awareness of responsibilities, comply with the law, sharing is as important as protecting) sit underneath every data-flow design.
National data infrastructure integration
HomeTest plugs into NHSE's strategic data architecture. NHS Login carries identity. NHS Notify carries communications. The Federated Data Platform (FDP) is being scoped as the events backbone. The Single Patient Record (SPR) is the canonical destination for some result types. Each of these carries its own IG framework, lawful basis, and controller designation that HomeTest has to align to rather than create afresh. How HomeTest's IG framework dovetails with FDP and SPR governance is still being scoped.
Open Q for SLT: legal directions and lawful basis
Whether HomeTest operates under direct lawful basis (commissioner consent), under legal directions issued to NHSE, or under a combination depending on configuration. The position is unresolved at SLT level and has implications for the data controller framework, the supplier contract architecture, and the assurance bar suppliers have to meet at admission.

What Ngozi needs from the framework

Clarity on controller designation per configuration, so the lawful basis question has a defensible answer per pathway. An IAO appointed and signed in before any go-live. Standard DSA templates that can be reused across commissioners without renegotiation per deployment. A clear interface with the strategic NHSE data infrastructure (NHS Login, NHS Notify, FDP, SPR) so HomeTest is not creating parallel governance. And a clean route for breaches and subject access requests so the operational IG load doesn't fall on a project team.

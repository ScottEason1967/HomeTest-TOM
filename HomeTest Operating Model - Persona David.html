The cast · meet David

David.

NHS Commercial
“Suppliers join the framework on the same terms. Commissioners buy from it on the same terms. That's what makes the model scale.”
Procurement and contracts lead. Holds the framework agreement and the catalogue rules. Cares about who can sell what, on what terms, and how exit and dispute work.

Why this role matters

Without framework administration, the model collapses into bilateral deals. David is the keeper of the rules: the published criteria, the max pricing, the call-off mechanics, the exit and dispute provisions. He's the one who decides whether a pattern is admitted, whether it can be amended, when it gets retired. The thing that makes the framework a framework rather than a list is David's role: consistency across commissioners, consistency across suppliers, consistency across configurations.

The framework is what David maintains. Without him, every commissioner is doing bilateral deals. The four-option Procurement Options Matrix sits in his territory. The Year 1 / Year 2 question (PSR, PA23 Framework, Dynamic Market, DPC) is his to recommend on. Whatever route gets picked, David is the role accountable for making it work consistently across deployments.

What drives him

An early NHS stint that scarred him. Brief role as a finance and procurement analyst in his late twenties on a major regional NHS procurement that got bogged down for two years. Met his now-wife on that programme , she was a senior nurse working in a service the procurement was meant to support and watched the supply chain failures land on her ward weekly. They still talk about how the commercial setup failed clinicians more than it failed suppliers.

A framework that became supplier capture. Years later he was on the team running a high-profile NHS framework that started clean and ended captured , three suppliers ended up controlling eighty per cent of the market and pricing rose every year. He learnt that commercial discipline isn't an end in itself; it's the only thing that stops capture. The discipline he brings to framework design now traces directly to that lesson.

What it adds up to. David doesn't think procurement is glamorous. He thinks it's the unsexy backbone of population health. Get it wrong and clinical fails. He's not in this for the procurement craft; he's in it because procurement is the leverage point that gets services to patients at scale. The Digital Therapeutics framework is the chance to do it once across multiple products without repeating the failure modes he's catalogued over twenty years.

What David is responsible for

The framework agreement itself: what suppliers sign, what commissioners draw against. The catalogue admission criteria: what makes a supplier eligible, what conformance evidence is required. The pricing architecture: three-tier structure (mobilisation fee, activity on cost per valid result, volume floor) with maximum pricing set nationally. The contract architecture: call-off mechanics, framework call-off templates, exit terms, dispute resolution. The procurement vehicle itself: NHSE-administered Open Framework under PA23, confirmed at the 22 June 2026 SLT playback. Within-PA23 market form (Open Framework chosen over Dynamic Market) settled at playback.

What sits in David's territory

David's territory is the framework as an instrument and the commercial machinery that runs it. Six things sit explicitly under his care, and each one has live work attached to it today.

Framework instrument and call-off architecture
The framework agreement itself: what suppliers sign, what commissioners draw against. Call-off mechanics, framework call-off templates, exit terms, dispute resolution. The thing that makes the framework a framework rather than a list of suppliers. This is the artefact that replaces MOUs at BAU.
Pricing architecture
Working structure is three-tier: mobilisation fee plus activity plus volume floor. Activity tier measured on cost per valid result (loaded for repeats, rejections, postal failures and abnormal-result handling), not flat per-kit pricing. Maximum pricing set nationally; commissioners negotiate below the ceiling. Flat per-kit was rejected; subscription was rejected at strategic level. The final activity-unit position is covered in the Future State team's Recommendation paper. Substantiation sits with Health Economist input, queued behind the PSA pathway workstream. PSA Active Surveillance is a separate model (Monitoring Episode, per-patient-per-year), not a variant of this question.
PSA Active Surveillance Monitoring Episode tariff
A designed tariff mechanism with a glide path (shadow 26/27 to dual-run 27/28 at 50% to live 28/29 at 100%) and five reconciliation mechanisms: bundled episode price, efficiency-factor offset, glide-path, capacity-release credits, ICB-level capitated AS top-up. Tariff modelling now in flight. Cost-analysis substantiation depends on health economist input.
Future-condition accommodation
How the framework admits new conditions (beyond HIV, PSA-AS, iron deficiency anaemia) without requiring a fresh procurement each time. Affects supplier admission criteria, call-off shape, capability requirements and pricing mechanics. Currently open at architectural level.
Critical Path future-state options paper
Consolidated paper for SLT setting out the procurement vehicle recommendation, framework structure, supplier admission approach, pricing and timeline implications. Target early June 2026 for SLT decision. One-off delivery item from the commercial workstream.
Sourcing strategy timeline
Document that locks the procurement route and framework administration decisions, and sets out the activity timeline to April 2027. Draft by mid-June 2026, final by end June. Gating document: everything downstream of the procurement decision waits on it.
Open Q for SLT: procurement vehicle within PA23
PA23 is settled as the procurement regime. The choice within PA23 (Open Framework, Dynamic Market, DPC Framework) is open. Coupled to minimum supplier count: an Open Framework can cap supplier numbers; a Dynamic Market cannot. A supplier cap rules out Dynamic Market. SLT decision needed to land the recommendation paper.
Open Q for SLT: framework administration route
NHSE direct, SBS, CCS or third-party. NHS Commercial preference is to outsource. SBS exploration in early stages. The Sourcing Strategy (draft v0.1 in flight) depends on this landing. Affects the BAU function shape sized on the Teamwork page.
Open Q for SLT: pathology supplier-mix model
NHS-pathology-first with private backstop, or parallel mixed-economy. Affects should-cost, EOI partner selection, framework structure and tariff modelling. A live commercial-workstream decision sitting at SLT level.
Open Q for SLT: supplier off-boarding mechanics
No precedent elsewhere in NHSE. PA23 provides the legal basis but operational mechanics (notice periods, evidence thresholds, appeals, patient continuity, sample-in-flight handling, residual contractual obligations) all need to be designed. Without it the framework locks in to first-mover suppliers by default.

What David needs from the framework

Pre-cleared supplier assurance through Joe's onboarding gateway, so call-offs draw on a known-good pool without per-deployment reassurance. Health Economist evidence to substantiate the pricing positions before NHSE Pricing engagement. SLT direction on procurement vehicle, framework administration route and pathology supplier-mix to close the gating decisions. Tariff treatment confirmed by NHSE Pricing for the PSA Monitoring Episode and (downstream) for the HIV pricing structure once the Recommendation paper position lands. A standing capability to admit new conditions without re-procurement, so the framework scales beyond the first three clinical areas without restarting.

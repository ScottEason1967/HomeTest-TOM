An operating model designed around real people.

Test at home. Get a clear result. Take action with confidence. Three patient archetypes: Abdul (CVKM screening, patient-initiated), Mark (PSA-AS, clinician-initiated) and Layla (HbA1c, GP-initiated). Served by the same architecture, the same cast, the same overlays.

Every operating model has to answer five questions. WHY it exists, at the centre. WHAT it is composed of, WHEN things happen, HOW people work day to day, and WHO is accountable around it.

Abdul, 38 Patient-initiated · CVKM Works full time, doesn't want to take time off for a screening clinic. Picks up an NHS App nudge, takes a BP check at home, lands in a CVKM Check Up if his risk score warrants it.
Mark, 65 Clinician-initiated · PSA-AS On active surveillance for low-grade prostate cancer. His consultant orders the PSA, the kit comes to the house, the result is reviewed before he ever hears about it.
Layla, 42 GP-initiated · HbA1c Working two jobs and raising kids. Keeps missing her diabetes review appointments. Her GP arranges an HbA1c kit at home; result lands in her record, and she's only contacted if action is needed.
How to read the model

Five questions. Nine people. One model.

WHY sits in the centre. WHAT, WHEN, HOW and WHO sit around it. Five questions, five pages. The same nine people thread through all of them.

WHY
Why does the model exist?
Purpose. Three lenses: the problem we solve, the economics that have to hold, why each audience engages. Patient, Commissioner, Supplier at the centre.
WHAT
What is it composed of?
Anatomy. Four levels: foundation (what every test type shares), overlays (what varies by pathway), patterns (named recipes that combine foundation + overlays), configurations (live deployments with specific suppliers and parameters). The foundation / overlay split is what lets one platform serve many conditions without rebuilding from scratch.
WHEN
When do things happen?
Lifecycle. Five-stage continuous loop: Shape, Assure, Contract, Fulfil, Measure. The same cast appears at each stage in different combinations.
HOW
How does it operate?
Operations. Seven swimlanes, one per actor (including a dedicated information governance lane). Foundation vs overlay colour coding inside the lanes shows what's universal vs what varies by configuration.
WHO
Who is accountable?
Governance. Decision rights, RACI tables, escalation routes. Ten open governance questions still pending SLT input.

Same nine people in every frame: three patient archetypes (Abdul, Mark, Layla) plus six operating roles (Sarah, Joe, Aisha, Priya, David, Ngozi). The questions are how you read the site. The people are who you're reading about.

The cast

Nine people. Three patient stories. One model.

An operating model is a set of relationships between people. Three patient archetypes: Abdul (CVKM screening, patient-initiated), Mark (PSA-AS, clinician-initiated) and Layla (HbA1c, GP-initiated). Alongside them, the five roles who run the model around them. Every page on this site is structured around what each plays.

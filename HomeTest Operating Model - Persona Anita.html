What Anita needs from the framework

Three things. First, a viable funding route at pathway level. HbA1c annual review is already QOF-incentivised for the diabetes register (DM006), and the diabetes prevention programme has its own routes; but neither is designed for home-collected sampling at scale. The route has to land before HbA1c can scale as a home-testing configuration. Second, IG that fits the GP-record integration shape (FHIR write-back, DSA between supplier and GP practice). Third, a buy mechanism that fits ICB-level commissioning and doesn't require every GP practice to sign separately.

The commissioner pitch in 60 seconds

The pitch Anita hears in the first ten minutes, before she decides whether the conversation is worth continuing. Direct answers to the questions she will ask. No aspiration, no architecture, no abstractions.

Where does the value land for me? GP appointment freed. Every annual HbA1c done at home is one fewer 10-minute practice slot consumed by routine monitoring, releasing primary-care capacity for the patients who clinically need to be in the room. Phlebotomy load lifted off practice nurses, who are scarce. Lab routine-monitoring volume moves off the daily collection. Register-completion rates go up because the appointment is no longer the barrier. The cashable saving per test is modest; the released capacity plus the equity reach into at-risk cohorts is the real economic move, and it shows up in QOF completion and diabetes-prevention referral metrics.
Who's going to pay me for this? Funding mechanism is still in flight. QOF (Quality and Outcomes Framework) is one route under consideration: HbA1c is already QOF-incentivised through DM006 (annual HbA1c for the diabetes register) and could be folded into an equivalent indicator for the at-risk register. NHS Diabetes Prevention Programme funding is another route. Neither is available within the 2027/28 tariff window for home-collected sampling at scale, so the working arrangement for the first deployments is bilateral commissioning at ICB or GP-federation level under the NHS Standard Contract. The wider primary-care contractual mechanism is unresolved at SLT level and on the open governance questions list.
How quickly can I get this in front of patients? Mobilisation is weeks not months for a practice that opts in. The patient cohort is already identifiable (at-risk register patients due their annual HbA1c review). The GP requests via existing electronic-prescribing pathways, same workflow as ordering a routine in-clinic blood. The kit arrives, the sample comes back, the result lands in the GP record ahead of the review consultation. The practice change is the consent conversation and the workflow tweak, not a new clinical pathway.
What if it doesn't work for my cohort? Exit terms sit in the call-off contract. The practice reverts to in-clinic phlebotomy at any point. HbA1c review is an annual cycle, so switching back to in-person testing is a service-level adjustment, not a clinical step-back. Supplier-switching within the pattern is supported via the framework's switching mechanism. No multi-year platform lock-in.
How is this different from what GPs already do? GPs already commission home-collected sampling in some pathways (HbA1c via Digital Health Checks with Thriva; HPV self-sampling for cervical screening; Hep-C via Preventx). What changes here is the framework wrapper: a single national platform brokering admitted suppliers, with results writing back to the EPR cleanly, an MI feed into the ICB-level commissioning view, and reach into the at-risk cohorts standard in-clinic provision misses (shift workers, carers, those who repeatedly miss appointments — Devi is the archetype). The novel piece for primary care is treating annual monitoring for a whole register as a candidate for home self-sampling at scale.

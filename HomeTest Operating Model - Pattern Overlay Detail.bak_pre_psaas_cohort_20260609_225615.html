Pattern overlays in detail.

Plain-English explanation of every Level 3 overlay across the four configurations.

The Level 3 stack on the Anatomical page shows nine overlay rows across four configurations. Each pill is short by design (HIV picks "Per-completed" for Commercial, PSA picks "Activity-based", and so on) but the labels don't carry their own meaning. This page explains what each overlay actually decides and what each configuration has picked in plain language.

Configurations: HIV is live Beta in Salford and East Sussex (solid commitment). PSA-AS is the Acute Use Case (Mark's journey, design firm, deployment prospective). iron deficiency anaemia is Use Case 3 (Layla's journey, GP-init, design phase only). Pharmacy-init is the fourth initiation pattern from the discovery taxonomy (working sketch only, not on Year 1 plan).

1

Initiation

What this overlay decidesWho triggers the test. The patient themselves, or a clinician acting on the patient's behalf. The initiation pattern shapes everything downstream: who orders, who pays, where the result lands, and how onward action is owned.
HIV
Live
Patient-initiated

The patient orders a test themselves through the NHS App. Triggered by symptoms, a screening invite, a peer recommendation, or self-initiated browse. No clinician request needed.

Example: Abdul (CVKM screening, patient-init via NHS App BP tool).

PSA-AS
Prospective
Clinician-initiated, patient-confirmed (acute)

Hospital clinical staff identify clinical suitability and set the intent to order. The patient confirms the order, address and timing. The kit is dispatched, the patient returns the sample, and the result returns to the requesting clinician. Repeat ordering carries the same confirmation step at each cycle.

Year 1 cohort: radiotherapy follow-up (men in post-treatment PSA surveillance after radiotherapy, monitored for biochemical recurrence) plus a monitoring-gap cohort (men referred to Urology with elevated PSA but no cancer diagnosis). Mark's journey is the radiotherapy-follow-up cohort.

Persona: Adam, consultant urologist

iron deficiency anaemia
Design
GP-initiated

The patient's GP arranges routine diabetes monitoring. The result returns to the GP record as part of the standard primary-care diabetes review cycle.

Layla's journey. Use Case 3 in the OBO list.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Pharmacy-initiated

A community pharmacist initiates testing as part of medication management (HRT, GLP-1, DMARDs / steroids, RA, etc.). Working sketch from the discovery taxonomy. Not on the Year 1 OBO list.

Underneath the clinician-initiated configurationsHomeTest sits underneath existing clinical recall and tracking systems where they already exist. Where a trust runs a condition-specific tracker for scheduling and recall, HomeTest is the remote-delivery layer beneath it, not a replacement. The tracker remains the source of truth for cadence and clinical management; HomeTest enables the kit, sample and result flow to happen remotely rather than in clinic.
Across clinician-initiated patternsPatient route choice is preserved. The patient retains the choice between in-person and home test at the point of order confirmation. The clinician sets the intent; the patient consents to the delivery route. This applies at every order in a repeat cycle, not only at first order.
2

Commissioning

What this overlay decidesWhich budget-holder pays for the service and on what contractual basis. The commissioner is the buyer in the model: the body that signs the call-off, owns the contract, and holds the budget.
HIV
Live
Local Authority · Sarah

The Local Authority commissions through its sexual health budget. NHS Standard Contract under the existing SBS Sexual Health Framework (Lot 1). Salford and East Sussex are the two live Beta deployments. Sarah is the commissioner persona.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Acute trust (Catherine) · monitoring-gap (Ruth, TBC)

Established cohort (radiotherapy follow-up). An acute trust holds the contract under the tariff envelope. Catherine is the commissioner persona; this is Mark's cohort.

Monitoring-gap cohort. Commissioner shape held open pending the cohort discovery output. Ruth is the persona slot. Live possibilities: acute trust, ICB, primary-care commissioning, joint arrangement, or central NHSE position.

NHS Online is the likely first customer at a central layer above the acute trust for the established cohort, establishing a one-to-many payment shape: NHS Online commissions, multiple suppliers serve through the platform.

iron deficiency anaemia
Design
ICB / GP · Anita

Primary-care commissioning route. ICB or GP practice as commissioner. Funding through QOF (Quality and Outcomes Framework) or outpatient tariff is being considered. Position depends on primary-care contractual mechanism, which is not available within the 2027/28 tariff window. Anita is the commissioner persona.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Pharmacy network / ICB

Community pharmacy network or ICB. Different governance regime under the Community Pharmacy Contractual Framework (CPCF). Open question about where this fits in the framework architecture.

3

Supply chain

What this overlay decidesWhich supplier delivers which components of the journey (kit dispatch, sample draw, lab analysis, sample return, result delivery). The supply chain pattern affects single vs multi-supplier framework design, accreditation reach, and where the operational risk sits.
HIV
Live
All-inclusive supplier

One supplier per pathway covers everything end-to-end: kit dispatch, sample analysis, result return. Single point of accountability. Preventx and SH:24 are the two live Alpha suppliers, each running the whole chain for their LA.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Specialist lab + logistics partner

A specialist lab handles the analysis; logistics may sit with a separate kit-fulfilment partner. Capillary draw equivalence to venous draw is being assessed by the Pathology SIG and the Royal College of Pathologists.

NHS-pathology-first vs parallel mixed-economy supply chain is a live commercial-workstream decision.

iron deficiency anaemia
Design
Lab-led

A pathology lab leads the chain; logistics integrate around the existing primary-care diabetes pathway. Working assumption pending Use Case 3 design work.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Local lab

Local pathology lab serving the pharmacy network. Working sketch. Practical mechanics not designed.

4

Commercial

What this overlay decidesHow the supplier gets paid and from which budget. Important: this is the payment to the supplier, not to the commissioner. The supplier delivers the test; the commercial pattern sets the payment shape and source.
HIV
Live
Per-completed-test, LA budget

The supplier is paid per completed test, not per kit dispatched. A kit only counts when the patient returns the sample, the lab analyses it, and the result is delivered. Incomplete journeys do not trigger payment.

Funded from the LA's sexual health budget through the ACE call-off in Alpha; new commissioning route for Beta. Volume-tiered maximum pricing applies.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Activity-based, acute envelope

Activity-based payment wrapped inside the acute provider tariff envelope. The supplier's revenue is tied to the volume of PSA monitoring episodes delivered, paid out of the acute trust budget.

Cost-stack still being modelled by the Health Economist.

iron deficiency anaemia
TBC
TBC

Genuinely undecided. Could be activity-based, episode-based, or absorbed into a primary-care tariff. Depends on the commissioning route landed (ICB vs GP practice) and primary-care contractual mechanism availability.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Per-review

Working assumption: a per-review fee covering the pharmacist consultation plus the test, mirroring the payment shape used for existing community pharmacy services (e.g. New Medicine Service).

Underneath all fourPricing structure is three-tier: mobilisation fee + activity (cost per valid result) + volume floor. Maximum pricing set nationally; commissioners run their own competitions for volume-based reductions. Flat per-kit pricing was rejected through the RFI and supplier forums; subscription rejected at strategic level. Pricing is referenced as A9 in the recommendation paper.
5

Tariff treatment

What this overlay decidesHow the patient gets the test free at point of care, and which national tariff (if any) the cost sits inside. Tariff treatment is what makes the configuration sustainable at scale: where the money comes from in the system, not how the supplier is paid.
HIV
Live
Commissioner-paid

Free at point of care for the patient. Funded via the LA test-and-treat budget through ACE call-off (Alpha) or new commissioning route (Beta). No national tariff line; sits inside LA commissioning budgets.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Acute envelope

Free at point of care. Diagnostic test sits inside the existing acute provider tariff.

PSA Active Surveillance Monitoring Episode tariff in design. Patient-Not-Present tariff being designed by NHSE Strategic Finance. Glide-path: shadow 26/27, dual-run 27/28 at 50%, live 28/29 at 100%. Five reconciliation mechanisms in play.

iron deficiency anaemia
Design
QOF / outpatient

Possibly QOF (Quality and Outcomes Framework, the primary-care payment mechanism) or outpatient tariff. Depends on commissioning route.

Primary-care contractual mechanism is not available within the 2027/28 tariff window; iron deficiency anaemia may fall outside that cycle and require alternative funding articulation.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
New code?

No existing tariff applies. Would need a new payment code under the Community Pharmacy Contractual Framework. Open question pending SLT scoping decision.

Common shape across configurationsTariff design itself sits outside the operating model as a separate workstream. The operating model's job is to make the dependency explicit. Configurations only land where the commissioner has a viable funding route at the pathway level.
6

Accreditation

What this overlay decidesWhich body certifies the lab or service component as fit to operate, and to what standard. Accreditation is the regulatory ticket: without it the lab cannot legally analyse NHS clinical samples, and the test result has no clinical validity.
HIV
Live
UKAS / ISO 15189

The UK Accreditation Service (UKAS) certifies the lab under ISO 15189, the international standard for medical lab quality. This is the standard NHS lab accreditation. Both Preventx and SH:24 labs hold UKAS accreditation against ISO 15189.

Mature methodology, no equivalence question to resolve.

PSA-AS
Open question
Capillary draw equivalence

The PSA-AS pathway uses a capillary draw (finger-prick) rather than a traditional venous draw. Whether capillary results are clinically equivalent to venous results for PSA is the open accreditation question.

Pathology SIG and the Royal College of Pathologists are assessing equivalence. Until that lands, the accreditation route for capillary PSA is not settled.

iron deficiency anaemia
Design
UKAS / ISO 15189

Same standard NHS lab accreditation as HIV. UKAS-certified labs under ISO 15189. Mature methodology, well-established for iron deficiency anaemia.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
GPhC + UKAS

Two accreditation regimes layered. The General Pharmaceutical Council (GPhC) accredits the pharmacist as a registered provider of the service. The UKAS / ISO 15189 accreditation still applies to whichever lab analyses the sample.

7

Sign-off

What this overlay decidesWhich clinical authority holds the clinical sign-off on the result and owns what happens next, especially on a reactive or abnormal result. Sign-off is where the clinical accountability for the patient lands once the test has been performed.
HIV
Live
LA sexual health service

The LA-commissioned sexual health service holds clinical sign-off. If the result is reactive, the supplier's clinical team makes contact first (per the Reactive Result clinical pathway: five follow-up attempts before escalation). Onward referral routes into the LA sexual health service for confirmatory testing, treatment and ongoing care.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Acute trust governance

Acute trust governance holds sign-off. The requesting consultant urologist sees the result first, interprets it against the patient's PSA history, and decides next steps directly with the patient. Mark's journey.

iron deficiency anaemia
Design
GP / diabetes team

The GP or practice diabetes team signs off on the result. Onward action sits in primary care: adjusting medication, referring to specialist diabetes services, or lifestyle intervention. Layla's journey.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Pharmacist + GP joint

Joint sign-off. The pharmacist does the initial review at the consultation. The GP holds responsibility for any onward action (medication change, referral). Hand-off pattern between pharmacist and GP still being designed.

8

Result handling

What this overlay decidesHow the result reaches the patient and what experience they have. Result handling shapes the patient interface: direct via NHS App, mediated through a clinician, or integrated into an existing clinical workflow.
HIV
Live
Patient-direct via NHS App

The result goes directly to the patient via the NHS App. For non-reactive results, this is the full journey.

For reactive results, the platform-side safety control withholds the user-visible release (status held at RECEIVED, db record set to RESULT_WITHHELD) and triggers the clinical follow-up pathway through the supplier. Reactive results are never given digitally.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Clinician-first

The result goes to the requesting clinician first. The clinician interprets it (especially against the patient's existing PSA history and trajectory) and discusses with the patient. The patient does not see the raw number before the clinician.

iron deficiency anaemia
Design
GP record

The result flows into the GP record through FHIR-based integration. The GP or practice nurse contacts the patient as part of the routine diabetes review cycle. The patient experience anchors on the existing primary-care review rhythm, not a stand-alone result delivery.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Mixed

Mixed model: pharmacist sees the result first and may share with the patient at the pharmacy consultation, or routes to the GP for onward action. Pattern still being designed.

9

IG / data controller

What this overlay decidesWho is the data controller for the patient's data at each step of the journey, and what consent or lawful basis applies. The IG overlay determines the DPIA shape, the DSA structure, and the controller / processor designation per configuration.
HIV
Live
Federated multi-controller

NHSE controls the App / Notify foundation. The Local Authority commissioning the sexual health service controls the testing purpose. The supplier acts as processor. DPIA, DSA and DPA executed before go-live.

Joint-controller ruled out. Federated controller model is the foundation pattern.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Federated multi-controller

Same foundation framework as HIV. Condition-specific DPIA. Some controller positions TBC pending SLT decisions (specifically around the acute trust controller designation and lawful basis).

iron deficiency anaemia
Design
GP-record DSA

The data flow integrates with the GP record. A Data Sharing Agreement governs the flow from supplier to GP system. The GP practice is controller for the GP record; NHSE and supplier roles still being mapped.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Pharmacy DSA

Pharmacy holds initial data; flow into clinical systems governed by a pharmacy-specific DSA. Different IG regime under the Community Pharmacy Contractual Framework.

Underneath all fourThe IG framework itself is foundation (built once, reused). What varies per configuration is the contents of the DPIA, the specific lawful basis and the controller designation per actor. The shape stays the same.

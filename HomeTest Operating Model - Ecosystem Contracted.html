4Contracted commercially

Direct commercial relationships

Either HomeTest or its commissioners hold these contracts. Five broad categories, each a distinct commercial relationship type. The acute trust partnership for PSA is the most recent addition.

Framework suppliers In flight
Kits, labs, end-to-end providers admitted under the framework instrument. Contracts run between commissioner and supplier with NHSE as framework holder. Status: active admission flow. Two PMEs live; supplier EOIs being processed; further suppliers in the funnel. Owner: Commercial Operations with NHSE Commercial.
Royal Mail In flight
Carrier leg. Volume discounts apply per shipper, not aggregated across suppliers. Engagement underway on a Health Samples package category. Status: engagement live. Fulfilment SLA under negotiation, including the requested NHS fast-lane against Tracked 24 reliability gaps. NHSE acts as collective bargaining entity, not buyer. Owner: Exec Advice & Partnerships with Commercial Operations and NHSE Commercial.
Pathology labs In flight
Where separately contracted (e.g. PSA specialist lab). Sample processing where the supplier model splits logistics from analysis. Status: live for HIV via all-inclusive supplier model. For PSA, specialist-lab arrangement in design; NHS pathology network alternative being modelled. Owner: Exec Advice & Partnerships with Clinical Safety Lead (dual position, SLT decision pending).
Acute trust partnership (PSA pathway) Selected
Royal Devon University Healthcare selected as the acute partner for PSA active surveillance (assessment score 92.75/100). Mobilisation from 1 June against a baseline of approximately 917 patients on the existing active surveillance cohort. Cambridge University Hospitals (88.0) and Somerset (77.0) sit as reserves. Monitor My Health caveat: RDUH's commercial arm sits outside partnership scope. Broader direction remains to engage all Trusts prospectively, not to single-source PSA on RDUH alone. Status: partner selected. Mobilisation 1 June. Partnership commercials and scope-of-work being finalised. Owner: Exec Advice & Partnerships with Commercial Operations and NHSE Commercial.
Advisory and capacity Blocked
Contracted advisory (e.g. Efficio) and contracted Health Economics capacity. NHS Supply Chain runs an adjacent POCT framework that is not a HomeTest contract but sits in the same supplier market. Status: Health Economist engaged at 2.5 days a week from 9 June 2026. Priority sequence set by Deputy Director DTx: HomeTest PSA pathway first, then HealthStore COPD, then standard cost / benefits model across both products. Owner: SRO.
